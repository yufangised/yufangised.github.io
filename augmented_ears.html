Augmented Ears / 2012 / CIID


Augmented Ears is a human enhancement concept that explores the idea of additional and augmented hearing senses in distance future scenario.

The overwhelming information in the modern cities that people experience every day has been seen as the backdrop in our exploration of future human senses and perception. Through augmented ears, people can experience the world around in a more emotional approach - blocking the eyesight while providing eight ears, which can filter and hear different frequencies of sound. This allows users to reduce distracting noise and design a personalized auditory experience, whether by picking up a single frequency or combining two to create a unique soundscape.


#design / #wearable

Hardware: Ardino

Coding: Processing / C++

Augmented Ears was designed and created by Helle Rohde Andersen and me during a one-week course in Performative Design at the Copenhagen Institute of Interaction Design (CIID). Fashion and aesthetics were key elements of this project. To showcase its design, we conducted a series of photoshoots in various urban settings.

The prototype was built by repurposing an old horse-riding helmet and combining it with a set of headphones. Each ear features a magnet at its center, allowing it to be attached to the "main ears" on the helmet. This attachment acts as a switch, triggering a specific frequency of sound mapped to that particular ear.

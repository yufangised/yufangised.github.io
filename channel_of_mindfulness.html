YUFAN WANG

CHANNEL OF MINDFULNESS / 2012 / CIID



Discover mindfulness in everyday life and inspire spiritual growth.

Bringing mindfulness into our daily routines is a key step for those seeking spiritual growth. Channel of Mindfulness offers a fresh approach to help beginners integrate mindfulness into their lives. The idea is to guide people to live in the moment by listening to meditative sounds from unique environments. With the assistance of a rolling gesture, users are encouraged to focus their attention on listening, cultivating mindfulness and awareness in the process.

Designed for those new to spiritual practice, Channel of Mindfulness provides an accessible starting point. It supports individuals who are curious about spirituality but may struggle with traditional practices like meditation due to modern distractions or personal challenges. As a first step, it helps users begin their spiritual journey and prepare for deeper exploration.

The design consists of two interconnected components: a small handheld device and an application running on an iPhone. The physical interaction happens on the device, where users can tune to find a channel, roll the device to listen, and experience physical feedback. Meanwhile, the iPhone handles tasks such as network connectivity, graphical interface, and sound processing.

The Device


The device is compact and portable, making it easy to carry. Whenever you want to clear your mind and create a mindfulness experience, simply take it out, connect it to your iPhone, and open the application. Use the tuning dial to find a sound channel around you, and roll the device smoothly at a pre-set speed (adjustable in the app's settings) to hear a clear and immersive sound track.

The M.Channel App


The iPhone application, called M.Channel, is designed to be simple and user-friendly. A brief instruction guide is available on the Info page, and the Settings page allows users to customize their meditation frequency and preferred rolling speed. Tuning to channels also requires patience. As users get closer to a channel, the TV snow effect and white noise gradually fade, and an image appears, providing basic information about the sound of the channel.

Users can access different sound channels according to their location, which is identified using GPS. New channels are updated over time, and the application sends notifications to keep users motivated and engaged.

User Testing


The positive feedback from the user testing sessions confirmed the validity of the concept. Users also provided valuable suggestions for improving the design, such as adding more weight to the rolling component and making the sound feedback more responsive to changes in rolling speed.

Prototyping


In the implementation phase, choosing the right material, weight, and form factor to ensure a comfortable feel in the hand was a significant challenge. Additionally, the technical aspects were complex, as seamless communication between the iPhone and the Arduino Lilypad had to be achieved.

Fireworks Latern / 2012 / Beijing Maker Space


Fireworks Lantern is a lantern crafted primarily from Printed Circuit Boards (PCB), designed to interact dynamically with ambient sound—particularly the sounds of fireworks during Chinese New Year celebrations. The lantern's LEDs respond to sound volume, lighting up in sync with specific frequency ranges to create a vivid, interactive display.

This project marks my initial exploration into creative applications of PCB technology to craft objects that go beyond functionality, embodying both aesthetic and cultural value. The Fireworks Lantern merges two iconic Chinese cultural element—lanterns and fireworks—into a single form, creating an interesting visual contrast between the tradition and the sense of technology.

Maker Faire Rome 2013

Fireworks Lantern had the incredible honor of being selected and invited to exhibit at Maker Faire Rome 2013, held from October 3 to October 7. During the event, it earned not just one but two awards: the "Maker of Merit" and the "People's Choice", reflecting both its innovation and the love it received from attendees.

YUFAN WANG

FUNCTION() / 2012 / CIID


Function() is an interactive installation that explores the concept of "responsiveness" while demonstrating one of the core principles of programming—the function—through tangible interaction and visual feedback.

The installation centers around the “Function Ball”, where users can stack six tangible function pieces, each uniquely designed with five strategically positioned holes. Light from a projector shines through these holes, triggering light sensors on the ball. Each piece activates a specific function, changing the behavior of the projected balls in real time.

Here are the six functions I created:


  • attraction(); - The balls move toward each other.
  • billiardBall(); - The balls bounce off one another like billiard balls.
  • colorFilter(); - The balls shift from black and white to random colors.
  • explode(); - The balls explode when they collide with the "Function Ball".
  • gravity(); - The balls are pulled downward, like they’re under gravity.
  • texture(); - The balls are covered with a textured pattern.

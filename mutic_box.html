YUFAN WANG

MUTIC BOX / 2011 / CIID


The Mutic Box is a haptic music player that simulates the tactile and auditory experience of a mechanical music box, evoking nostalgic memories through sound and haptic feedback.

Users interact with the Mutic Box through a rolling wheel that generates a slight rumble (simulated by a DC motor) as each note is played. The wheel is connected to a custom music decoder through a gear system. The decoder is made from acrylic and contains 28 conductive holes, each corresponding to a note in the melody. As the decoder rolls, it sends signals to an Arduino controller, which processes them in Max-MSP to play the corresponding notes.

This project was the result of a two-day hackathon collaboration with my classmate Daim Yoon at CIID.
